idiioms 01

  • 1. a blessing in disguise

    something that seems to be bad or unlucky at first, but which you later realize is good or lucky

    Be positive, this could be a blessing in disguise, use the time gained wisely.

  • 2. a picture is worth a thousand words

    used to say that it is often easier to show something in a picture than to describe it with words

    Its easier to learn how a machine works from pictures rather than descriptions, since a picture is worth a thousand words.

  • 3. a piece of cake

    very easy task; very simple work

    They said the test would be difficult, but it was a piece of cake – I’ll pass with no problem at all.

  • 4. actions speak louder than words

    doing is better than just speaking; better to do something rather than just talk

    A good leader does not tell his team how things should be done, he shows them; after all, actions speak louder than words.

  • 5. add insult to injury

    to make a bad situation worse; to worsen an unfavorable or uncomfortable situation

    I was already getting late for work stuck in the traffic, and to add insult to injury, I was stopped by the police for speeding.

  • 6. bark up the wrong tree or be barking up the wrong tree

    to make a wrong choice; take a wrong approach to something

    I am not the person who spread those rumours about you, you are barking up the wrong tree.

  • 7. beat around the bush or beat about the bush

    avoid talking about the main topic; avoid the important point

    Quit beating around the bush and tell me what you really want.

  • 8. bite off more than one can chew

    attempt to do something which is hardly achievable; try to do more than one is able to do

    It feels like I bit off more than I could chew when I promised to complete this worksheet in one day.

  • 9. bite the bullet

    to avoid showing fear or discomfort in an unpleasant situation; to be brave and face a difficult situation

    Mary has to learn to bite the bullet and face her fears of flying.

  • 10. break a leg

    good luck; to wish something luck especially before a performance

    You have an exam tomorrow? Break a leg!
